Melbourne couple stop off at Mcdonalds after $95k wedding

 Posted on June 30, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  732 words  |  Martina Birk

Newlyweds Zeina and Gabriel Chaya may have spent a whopping $95,000 on the wedding of their dreams but having fun was the order of the day.

The Melbourne pair made a pit-stop at McDonald's in St Kilda on their special day, making a long-held childhood dream of Mrs Chaya's come true.

Speaking to Daily Mail Australia, the bride joked that a visit to the golden arches on her wedding day had been something she'd been thinking about since she was a child.

'Ever since I was a little girl I'd always dreamt of going to Maccas on my wedding day,' she giggled.

The Melbourne-based couple, both 29, along with their wedding party, were in-between wedding photo sessions when they decided to stop off for their favourite fast food.

It had been a long day for the bride, who said she been up at 4am that morning to begin preparations for the long day ahead.

'Nervousness and excitement meant I'd barely eaten anything all day. By the time we got to Maccas it was close to 4.30,' she said.

Preparation for Mrs and Mr Cheya's wedding had begun at 4am that morning

Although the bride couldn't have looked more beautiful in a full-length designer gown by Pronovias and Christian Louboutin heels, she said she was happy fuel up on a McChicken burger at McDonald's in St Kilda before being whisked off to her next photo shoot.

She explained that despite many months of planning which had gone into the couple's fairy-tale themed wedding, she was delighted that among the extravagance, the couple made time to do something simple.

The relaxed bride said the pair go to Maccas at least once a fortnight so the pit stop was 'no big deal'

Mrs Cheya said having a bridesmaid on hand helped with any potential 'Maccas mishaps'

The pair, who've known each other since they were 12, were good mates in high school. 

They'd maintained their friendship over the years, but only began dating a few years ago, Mrs Cheya said.

Two years in, her then-husband to be proposed and the two started preparations for a traditional wedding celebrating their Lebanese culture. 

Mrs Cheya had two dresses on the day, one for the ceremony and another fitted dress for the reception

Wanting their special day to be opulent and elegant, the newly-married couple spent a staggering $95,000 to make sure their perfect day was a beautiful experience.

Along with a reception that included a four-course catered meal and full bar for four hundred guests, the couple splashed out on two wedding gowns, four bridesmaids dresses, bespoke tailoring for the groom and suits for four groomsmen.

They also hired the Luxor Function Centre that allowed them to style to an 'enchanted garden' theme, pyrotechnics and a full band.

It's not surprising the cost might bring a tear to the eye of many as it's close to three times what the average Australian couple spend on their wedding, according to EasyWeddings.com.au.

The honeymoon was equally as glamorous with the pair jetting off to New York, the Bahamas and Mexico for four weeks to take some much needed time out.

Alongside taking in the sights, the pair naturally made time for Mcdonald's but  the overseas version didn't quite live up to expectations, Mrs Cheya said.

'We dropped in for Maccas in New York, but it wasn't the same as Aussie Maccas,' she said.
