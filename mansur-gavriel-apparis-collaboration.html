Mansur Gavriel x Apparis Collaborate on Fall Coats and Bags Exclusive

 Posted on September 18, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  591 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Mansur Gavriel didn’t have a cake to celebrate its 10th birthday at New York Fashion Week in September. Instead, the accessories brand took over a Lower Manhattan street for a farm-to-table block party. Long tables filled with bite-size fruits and veggies channeled the spot on Mansur Gavriel’s mood board dedicated to fresh produce. (“We’re very inspired by nature, fruits and vegetables, and food,” co-founder Rachel Mansur told Bazaar.) Unbeknownst to the fashion editors invited over for snacks and a live runway show, the event also gave an edible hint about a bigger project. Today, Bazaar can exclusively reveal that Mansur Gavriel is releasing a capsule of vegan fall coats and color-coordinating bags with Apparis.

The Mansur Gavriel x Apparis lineup is inspired by the hues of a juicy edible arrangement and made with bio-based materials. It begins with a burgundy, leatherlike trench crafted from recycled apple skins and a coordinating rendition of Mansur Gavriel’s Candy bag. There’s also a mid-length faux-fur coat in a berryish hue and a short faux-fur jacket in an upbeat, bubblegum pink. Each coat has a fuzzy rendition of Mansur Gavriel’s classic mini bucket bag to match, and all four pieces are made from Apparis’s signature corn-based faux fur. The collection’s colors are each named for a fruit—rhubarb, blackberry, and guava—to wink at the line’s origins.

The designers began brainstorming around playful-yet-neutral colors and expanded to their five choice silhouettes, allowing them to speak directly to the New York women they design for. (The teams offered up Emily Ratajkowski, Jenna Lyons, and Katie Holmes as real-world style references.) “It’s a very curated and small capsule, which I think for us was intentional,” Apparis founder Lauren Nouchi said.

The collaboration’s coats set traditional materials aside without losing the charm of classic fall outerwear (that is, sumptuous textures and an equally rich palette). As for the bags, they provide the ease and functionality Mansur Gavriel is known for with more eco-conscious materials. The apple-based leather is carbon neutral, while making the corn-based fabric emits up to 60 percent fewer greenhouse gases than the process of creating traditional faux furs.

Apparis has always been 100 percent animal free. Vegan design is newer for Mansur Gavriel; the brand released an apple leather tote bag earlier this year. This initial test brought in equal praise from the designers’ vegan friends and anonymous shoppers alike, Rachel Mansur said. All that positive feedback led the Mansur Gavriel designers to contact Nouchi and set out on a more ambitious, eco-conscious collaboration.

The apple leather coat is a first for Apparis and a highlight for all three designers. “It felt very on-brand that [a coat and bag are] made from apple waste, because the apple is just this beautiful, iconic fruit,” Mansur said. Co-founder Floriana Gavriel added: “The apple leather is a really beautiful material. It has a really nice texture, and it takes colors really well.”

Groceries can’t take full credit for the color scheme. The trench coat and matching bag’s shade coordinates with a trend dominating fall 2023 collections: “Red leather was all over the runway,” Nouchi acknowledged. This collaboration’s version is much more appetizing.
