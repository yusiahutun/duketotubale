Plus-size model Charli Howard posts a naked selife

 Posted on February 1, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  702 words  |  Martina Birk

When Charli Howard showed someone a nude selfie she had taken, she was told 'don't think you should post that.'

But the 26-year-old Brit, famous for having quit an unnamed modelling agency in 2015 over 'unobtainable beauty standards,' isn't one to listen when someone tells her not to show off her body. 

New York-based Charli went ahead and posted the photo, showing her kneeling on a bed completely nude except for a towel on her head.

Body proud: Plus-size model Charli Howard shared a nude selfie on Tuesday after someone had told her she shouldn't

Hitting out: Charli wrote in the caption for the racy snap that she was snubbing the unnamed person's advice

Curvy beauty: She finished the posts with the captions #IloveMyBody and #LoveYours

She takes the selfie from the side, hiding her breasts with a strategically placed arm and showing off her natural curves.

In the caption she wrote: '"Don’t think you should post that" well guess what you’re in for a treat bbz cause I think I look FIYAHHHHH!!!!'

She finished the caption with a par of hashtags including #IloveMyBody and #LoveYours. 

Since that infamous day in 2015, Charli has established herself as an advocate for body positivity, regularly celebrating her curves, rolls, cellulite and even her stretchmarks. 

Back then, she was a petite size 6, had spent hours in the gym and was eating orange juice-soaked cotton wool when her modelling agency told her she would not be booked for a job because she was too big to fit into a pair of designer trousers.

Claim to fame: Charli gained attention back in 2015 when she publicly called out an unnamed modelling agency for telling her she was 'too big' at a tiny size 6

Then and now: She explained that she battled eating disorders for years and even passed out on a set because she wasn't eating in an effort to be thinner

Living life: The 26-year-old has said that she is 'proof' of how 'being thin does not make your life better'

Instead of feeling her usual sense of shame, model Charli Howard was consumed by anger.

On the way home she wrote a Facebook post in which she stated: 'Here's a big F*CK YOU to my (now ex) model agency for saying that at 5ft 8in tall and a UK size 6-8, I'm 'too big' and 'out of shape' to work in the fashion industry.'

Unsurprisingly the Londoner's 2015 post quickly went viral.

Almost two years on, the model who is now also a body-positive activist is again highlighting the issue ahead of the release of her memoir called Misfit which is aimed at young adults which charts the eating disorders and anxiety she suffered for 20 years.

Up and up: The Londoner, who now lives in New York, has become known for her frequent posts about body positivity

Honest portrayal: In her posts, she has celebrated her body's curves, stretch marks and cellulite

Getting known: She currently has more than 110,000 followers on Instagram

'I'd eat an apple a day, and I still wasn't thin enough for them,' she told The Observer earlier this year.

She claimed that she even fainted on a shoot from eating so little and a photographer simply fanned her with his hands and told her: 'But you look great.'

The New York City resident now regularly makes a point on social media to show off her natural form and to tell her followers that there is nothing wrong with having curves or a bit of extra weight – or a backside covered in cellulite.

Last April she took to Instagram to show just how far she has come since learning to accept her curves.

Along with a before and after photo she wrote: 'Look how far I've come!! I may be heavier than I was, but I'm happier than I've ever been, and I never, EVER want to go back to that miserable and lonely time.'
