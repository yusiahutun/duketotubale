How to Style Flare Jeans: Celebrity Stylist Reveals 7 Looks

 Posted on June 20, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1610 words  |  Martina Birk

When it comes to fashion, the old adage holds true: everything old is new again. And while some styles — like the 90s pairing of belly-magnifying high-waisted pants with short tops or curve-erasing oversized blazers from the 80s — are better left in the past, we’re seeing the return of one that we can get on board with: flare jeans from the 70s. We’re not talking full-on bell bottoms, but a more modernized version of the flowy-bottom pant style. The leg-elongating, hourglass-highlighting design has been seen on celebrities like Heidi Klum and Padma Lakshmi. Even better? We asked celebrity stylist how to style flare jeans so that they look ultra flattering for any occasion. Here, seven looks you’re sure to love.

Why are flare jeans flattering on women over 50?

As women, the struggle to find the perfect jean for our figure is universal. We want a pair that carves out curves, molds to our shape, hides flaws and trouble spots and has some stretch; after all, you want to feel comfortable in the more casual piece of clothing. To the rescue: flare jeans like the ones 53-year-old Padma Lakshmi is donning above. They skim over belly bumps, hug legs to pull the eye up and down for a taller, leaner look and flare out the bottom to balance out hips, explains celebrity stylist Eric Himel, who has worked with Giuliana Rancic and Tia Mowry.

The key to getting the most out to the slimming jean is how you style them. “Flare jeans can elongate a leg, especially when you get the proportion correct with the top,” says Himel. “Think of the 1/3 and 2/3’s rule: your top should take up 1/3 of your silhouette and your jeans 2/3s.” For example, when putting an outfit together with flare jeans, opting for a shorter top helps give the figure more balance. “Too long of a shirt can add unnecessary bulk,” he explains.

Also great? Even if you have a more ruler shape, the flared-at-the-bottom style helps create the illusion of curves.

2. How to style flare jeans for a night out

These days, the casual bottoms can be easily dressed up for a dinner date or office party. Simply pull on leg-slimming dark wash flares and pair it with a sexy, yet elegant red one-shoulder satin top. The bare shoulder tastefully shows off some skin while still remaining chic. And, the length of the shirt cuts off right above the jeans, following Himel’s 2:1 pants to top ratio.

Finish it off with a pair of comfy ballet flats (we have been loving these ones from Aerosoles!) and black and gold accessories, like a crossbody bag and pair of earrings.

3. How to style flare jeans for happy hour

A cocktail hour with friends, family or coworkers is not only a chance to catch up and unwind with a glass of wine (or two), but it’s also the perfect opportunity to get dressed up in a fun and cute way. Sure, we know that wearing head-to-toe black is slimming , but adding a pop of color, like this season’s hottest color deep red, is a bold way to add pizazz to the all-black look. Himel suggests pairing black flare jeans with a pointed toe shoe, such as these pointed toe booties. “A pointed toe is narrow — so it gives the illusion of your legs looking thinner and longer.” Plus, he adds, “a flared jean looks great with a heel — your choice if it’s a low, mid or high heel. And I am just loving a pointed mid-heeled boot right now with a flare jean.” The two together create a double leg-flattering effect.
