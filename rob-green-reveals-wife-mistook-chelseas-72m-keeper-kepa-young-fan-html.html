Rob Green reveals how his wife mistook Chelsea's 72m keeper Kepa for a young FAN

 Posted on July 1, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  884 words  |  Martina Birk

Former Chelsea goalkeeper Rob Green has revealed how his wife once mistook the club's £72million record-breaking shot-stopper Kepa Arrizabalaga for a young fan who was looking for an autograph. 

Spaniard Kepa arrived at Stamford Bridge from Athletic Bilbao in 2018 with huge expectations having become the most expensive goalkeeper in world football to replace the Real Madrid-bound Thibaut Courtois.

But Kepa, then 23, struggled to adapt to the Premier League and was guilty of a number of high-profile errors – falling out with first boss Maurizio Sarri and later being dropped by Frank Lampard following a string of clangers.

Green – then third-choice goalkeeper after Kepa and Willy Caballero – admitted there was a lot of pressure on a player who was so young, and recalled how his wife spotted him at their hotel shortly after he first signed and thought he was a child. 


'I tend to forget how young he was when he signed, and he went from his hometown club to Chelsea as the world's most expensive goalkeeper,' he told football.london.

'He was still just a youngster really. I remember I was at the Chelsea Harbour Hotel after I signed and it was the summer holidays. My wife turned to me and said, "I think there is a fan here. He's in a Chelsea tracksuit so he'll probably be coming over to say hello. He's the young lad over there with his mum and dad".

'I looked over and then said, "Oh no, that's the most expensive goalkeeper in the world. That's Kepa".

There was huge pressure on Kepa after arriving at Stamford Bridge for his big price tag

'It was easy to understand why my wife would look at him as just a young lad. That is what he was. So to have the chance to take a step back [over the past year] and to look at the overall picture [was important].'

Kepa played 54 times in his first season under Sarri – helping the team reach the Carabao Cup final and winning the Europa League – but played fewer games the following campaign after Lampard dropped him for Caballero.

And the year after the club decided to upgrade on him and bought Edouard Mendy from Rennes - and the Senegal international has been first choice ever since.

Green feels Kepa was at his best in his first season when he was in a Chelsea side that focused on total possession of the ball - while Lampard's football left the team more open at the back, which in turn hurt Kepa's displays and confidence. 

But Kepa struggled to impress as Chelsea's No 1 and was guilty of some high profile errors

'Under Sarri, he came into a Chelsea side that was focused on controlling the game, controlling the ball, getting Jorginho to play 600 passes or whatever,' said Green - who will appear in BT Sport's goalkeeping documentary The Special 1. 

'That was the focus of the game. Like it or lump it, love it or hate it, that's what Sarri's game was about.

'With that, you have an element of control within the goalkeeping world that certain things are going to happen within a game. So your focus is on your own game.

'Frank's game took it away from that. It was a lot more exciting, there were 5-2s with Tammy scoring hat-tricks and there being two own goals, there was a 2-2 with Valencia that I did the commentary for and we were exhausted just doing that!

Eventually he became second choice with Frank Lampard signing Rennes' Edouard Mendy

'If you're playing in games like that week in, week out while trying to also implement your own game, it's very difficult as a goalkeeper. It was also something he'd probably never come across before.

'Now he has come back into the Chelsea side under Tuchel, who moved to a back three because it gives control, who has Jorginho back in the centre, and all of a sudden he is looking back to his old self.

'So there are a lot of extenuating circumstances in there. If you asked Kepa how he feels, he'd probably say exactly the same. But when the game around you is so, so different, it lends itself to a more settled and comfortable goalkeeper.'
