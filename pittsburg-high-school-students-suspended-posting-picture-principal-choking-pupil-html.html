A fight that broke out on a high school campus last week saw ten students being suspended over a photograph, taken during the brawl, of the principal holding a student in a headlock, it emerged today.

The picture shows the principal of Pittsburg High School in California, Todd Whitmire, restraining the 15-year-old. The picture was posted online and was shared around social media sites by her classmates.

The student, Ashley Johnson, is now in a neck brace and said she was not fighting with anyone but lying on the ground.

Incriminating picture: Principal Whitmire is seen holding student Ashley Johnson around the neck in a choke hold during a fight

Principal Whitmire said: 'It certainly is not a description of what actually happened. When I was restraining her she fell to the ground'

Principal Whitmire said the picture is being taken out of context and is not an accurate depiction of events.

'It certainly is not a description of what actually happened,' Whitemire told Fox25. 'I think the disappointing aspect of it is the comments that associate the postings, ranging from racist comments to derogatory comments.'

He suspended anyone who shared the picture online and added any disparaging comments.

But students argue it is a violation of their first amendment rights and they were simply exercising freedom of speech.

'We post what we want and they should not get involved with what we post. If it's hurting them probably yeah but I don't think it was right to suspend them,' one student said.

Two other people were suspended for taking part in the fight, bringing the total to 12.

Student: Ashley Johnson is in a neck brace following the incident and she claims she was not fighting but lying on the ground

'Victim': Principal Whitmire said the problem was the reposting, the retweeting, and assigning negative comments to the picture and creating a hostile environment for Ashley

Whitmire says he tried to restrain Ashley because she refused to stop fighting and she slipped and fell.

'I had separated her and she began struggling and I was pushing her away to get her away from the area and she fell down,' he told the Contra Costa Times.

Other members of staff are backing up the principal's version of events.

Lt Ron Raman said: 'During (the melee), staff members were pulling people away from one another. The principal tried to pull a student away and she fell and he fell down with her.'

No charges are being filed against Principal Whitmire.
