Two pals turn bingo into wild night out selling over 1.5m tickets across UK

 Posted on May 26, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  355 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Two pals have reinvented bingo, turning the grannies’ favourite into a boozy, wild night out.

Raucous Bongo’s Bingo has surged in popularity across the country.

Traditional bingo is still at the heart of the night but punters are also treated to table dancing to the Venga-boys, giant fluffy unicorns, and cereal-eating contests.

Friends Jonny Bongo – real name Jonny Lacey – and Joshua Burke, both 33, started the event in April 2015 “as a bit of laugh”.

They have since sold more than 1.5million tickets to nights across the UK and employ more than 50 people.

Former pub quiz host Jonny said: “When Josh and I started Bongo’s Bingo we didn’t think many people really believed it would ever happen or become as crazy as it is.

“Even now it is sometimes really surreal if I take a step back to consider it all.”

There are dance-offs between games, rave-style music and laser shows with top DJs – and lots of drinking.

Guest callers have included Baywatch star David Hasselhoff, singer Jo O’Meara from S Club 7, and even Labour leader Jeremy Corbyn.

Footballers, pop stars and Olympians have been among the guests. Former Man United star Wayne Rooney’s wife Coleen wrote that she “had a ball” on a girls’ night out at an event in Liverpool.

Jonny and Joshua host events in more than 30 towns and cities across the country including Manchester, London and Blackpool.

And their success has now spread globally to Australia, Ibiza, Dubai and France. The shows sell out months in ­advance, drawing in revellers aged 18 to 92.

Now the ­ambitious duo aim to crack America next year.

Regulars praised the nights. One said: “One minute you’re raving, then you’re playing bingo to win anything from a sex toy to £650, then you’re singing school hymns.

“Mental is what the night is.”
