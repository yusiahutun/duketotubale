'I almost punched Ronaldinho after playing for England in gutting World Cup loss to Brazil'

 Posted on September 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  462 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Former England footballer Danny Mills once shared a funny story about how he almost punched Brazilian star Ronaldinho

The incident came after Ronaldinho scored his famous free-kick against England 22 years ago. Mills played in all of England's matches at the 2002 World Cup in South Korea and Japan due to an injury to first-choice right-back Gary Neville prior to the tournament.

In the quarter-finals, well-backed England came up against Brazil, who ultimately defied the pre-competition odds to win the World Cup, and Ronaldinho scored by lobbing the ball over David Seaman's head.

Ronaldinho didn't end the day happy though, as he got a red card for stamping down Mills' shin. And the former Leeds defender has since admitted he nearly hit Ronaldinho when they met after the match. Both players had to go for drug tests at the stadium in Japan with two other players, Rio Ferdinand and Cafu.

Mills said: told talkSPORT: "We walk in, Cafu's in there, Ronaldinho's in there. Ronaldinho had obviously been sent off for a debatable foul on myself, but he was laughing and joking, and to be honest, he was a little bit much, you know?
