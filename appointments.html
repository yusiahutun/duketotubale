SUN Hung Kai Insurance Consultants Limited has appointed Kenneth Mok as director.

He will be responsible for serving the company's major corporate clients and will assist in overseeing the company's day-to-day operations. ANSETT Australia has appointed Robert Mills as regional marketing manager, responsible for Hong Kong, Taiwan and South Korea.

Mr Mills will oversee aspects of marketing such as advertising and public relations in the lead up to the launch of the airline's Hong Kong service on September 10.JARDINE CMG Life Assurance Limited, Hong Kong's fourth largest insurance company, has appointed Ip So-lan as senior manager of information technology.

Ms Ip is responsible for developing, maintaining and upgrading a fully integrated business administration and support system.

Prior to joining the firm, Ms Ip spent six years in a similar capacity with American International Assurance Limited. THE Hong Kong Institute of Personnel Management has appointed Richard Lam as project manager.

He will be responsible for developing and co-ordinating major projects, in particular the 1996 World Congress on Personnel Management, the institute's annual conference, and the activities of the institute's Asia Pacific group.

He was previously fund-raising co-ordinator for Oxfam Hong Kong. ROTHSCHILD Asset Management (Singapore) Limited has appointed Jill Smith as the senior fund manager for Singapore, Malaysia and Indonesia.

Ms Smith will also assume responsibility for servicing Rothschild Asset Management's private clients in the Asian region.

NATIONAL Mutual Insurance Co Ltd has appointed Steve Hui as general manager of finance.

He has 19 years' experience in the finance industry and public services.

He has been with the National Mutual Group for 11 years.

In his new capacity in Hong Kong, Mr Hui will be responsible for management, statutory and investment accounting, auditing, financial control, taxation and planning. DANIEL Lam has been promoted to area manager of northeast Asia of London International Group, which manufactures consumer products such as Marigold gloves.

He has over 18 years' experience in marketing in Hong Kong, Taiwan and China.

CAMPBELL Soup Company has appointed a new management team for its businesses in Asia and Australia.

David Wells has been named regional vice-president and group managing director for the Asia Pacific.

Mark McCallum has been appointed managing director for Campbell Asia, while Michael O'Brien becomes managing director for Campbell mushrooms' division.

Mr Wells, 36, will be relocating from Hong Kong to Melbourne.

Mr McCallum, 39, will relocate from Melbourne to Hong Kong and be responsible for business in Southeast Asia.

HOLIDAY Inn Worldwide has made Steven Parker vice-president of strategic planning.

Mr Parker joins the Asia Pacific division from the company's Europe-Middle East-Africa region where he was director of strategic development.

Lim Lay-choo has been promoted to vice-president - general counsel, and Eric Lim becomes director of trade sales and planning.

BARRY Macdonald, partner at Coopers and Lybrand, has been elected president of the Canadian Chamber of Commerce in Hong Kong.

The chamber is an independent, non-profit organisation formed in 1977 and has more than 900 members, making it the largest chamber outside Canada. ANGELA Hui has joined Banque Paribas as assistant general manager in charge of the commodities and structured finance department in Hong Kong.

Connie Chan has been promoted to the head of the commodities department. KPMG Peat Marwick Hong Kong has appointed John Zabriskie as principal.

He joined KPMG in 1979 and became a partner in 1990.

In Hong Kong, he will help United States' multinational clients with operations in Asia, and provide services required by US clients establishing business ventures in China.

Sam Ranzilla becomes US securities partner in Hong Kong.

He will provide service to Asian corporations seeking financing in the US capital markets.

DENTAL Hall has appointed Sheena Brand as a consultant in the firm's regional construction group based in Hong Kong.

LYNN Ogden has been elected as a partner of the executive search firm, Korn/Ferry International (HK) Ltd.

She joined the firm in 1988 as a senior associate and, prior to her promotion, was a principal.

Ling Li has been made principal of the company, while Joyce Wong has become research associate.
