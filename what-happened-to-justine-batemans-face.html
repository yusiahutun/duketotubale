What Happened To Justine Bateman's Face?

There is nothing wrong with Justine Batemen's face in the cosmetic sense, but her fans are saddened that she is aging. 

On the other hand, Bateman has not only embraced it but has written a book about aging called Face: One Square Foot of Skin, published by Akashic Books in 2021.

The book is a compilation of fictitious stories that explores how men and women have grown to believe that older women's looks are undesired, unsightly, and something that has to be changed out of fear and ingrained evolutionary patterns.

For example, Faith, a 48-year-old former advertising executive who appears as one of Bateman's characters, leans down and catches a glimpse of her face reflected on a tabletop.

She is appalled by this fresh perception of her drooping skin.  Although she was conscious that her face was no different than it had been an hour earlier, she was also aware that it had crossed over and couldn't undo it or stop her confidence from waning.

The book shows the reader the various underlying factors for society's frequently unfavorable opinions about women's older faces based on the author Justine Bateman's experiences with older faces and those of the other women and men she interviewed.

By doing this, Bateman challenges the widespread belief that older women's appearances must be fixed and urges us to stop valuing people according to how well their faces look.

Bateman makes the case that a woman's confidence should increase as she ages rather than being devastated by society's false perception of that one square foot of skin with passionate writing and a laser-sharp eye.
