And finding a formula that won’t sink into fine lines and visibly age the skin can be extra tricky. So we talked to top makeup artists to find the best drugstore foundations that wear well on aging skin and create a stunning finish you can count on to look great in any light. Ready your virtual shopping cart with our picks that are all under $18! 

What can foundation do for women over 40?

Put simply: a lot. Foundation can be a part of a makeup routine at any age, thanks to its ability to erase areas of darkness and give you a great base for the rest of your makeup to shine. But it becomes even more useful as the years go by. 

According to celebrity makeup artist and founder of New York City’s Tomy Rivero Beauty Lab, Tomy Rivero, “As we age our skin gets thinner, showing more blue tones of our veins and discoloration from years of sun exposure.” And, he says, “Using a lightweight base with medium coverage can make you look not only more youthful but less tired and even-toned!”

What to look for in a drugstore foundation for mature skin

“I recommend a lightweight to medium buildable coverage formula that has some luminosity to it,” says Rivero. This will help blur fine lines. His tip? “Look for keywords like ‘hydrating,’ ‘glow,’ and ‘dewy finish’ when choosing the formula.”  

Makeup artist Elaina Badro, who has worked with Carrie Underwood and Christina Millian, also suggests looking for a foundation with a thinner consistency than you might have gone for in your younger years. She explains, “The less ‘cakey,’ the better. Typically, mature skin tends to be dryer.” That means makeup has more of a tendency to crease on older skin, which can enhance wrinkles. That’s why, she says, “it’s best to apply a foundation that has a satin or dewy finish. This allows for a subtle glow while hydrating the face.”

Another key? “I suggest looking for formulas that contain some elements of skincare,” says Nyssa Green, an Emmy-winning makeup artist and founder of the Green Room Agency in Atlanta. “In addition to sunscreen, many foundations now contain ingredients like squalene, hyaluronic acid, argan oil and licorice root,” she says. This means your makeup will double as a treatment as these ingredients ward off sun damage, hydrate skin, brighten areas of discoloration and reduce redness.

How to find the most flattering foundation shade

As for the shade you choose, Rivera has a rule of thumb: “As we age, color on our face tends to make us look healthier and energetic.” So you can try going one shade darker when choosing your foundation to add youthful color back to skin. He adds, “always make sure to bring down the colors to your neck and ears for a seamless match!” 

But to find your perfect color match, it’s best to swatch shades at the jawline down to the neck and go with the one that seamless blends in. It’s also help to determine your skin’s undertone when selecting a foundation as many shade ranges are labeled cool, neutral and warm. (Click through to learn how to determine your skin undertone.)

The foundation formula to avoid for mature skin

What not to look for, according to Green? “Mineral formulas are not the friend of mature skin. The formulas can come off looking shiny or greasy … or in some cases, the ‘minerals’ can look like big chunks of glitter!”

How to apply foundation on aging skin

  • Rivero says your first step is to exfoliate for a smooth surface. 
  • Next, apply moisturizer to hydrate and “fill in” any fine lines.
  • Then, “use a damp sponge to press in the foundation for the most natural finish.”  Stippling it on this way allows you to build the coverage you want without creating a heavy, cakey look.
