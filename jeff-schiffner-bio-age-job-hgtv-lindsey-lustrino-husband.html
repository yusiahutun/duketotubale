Jeff Schiffner Job

Jeff Schiffner has a LinkedIn profile. Based on that, he has been working for Goldman Sachs as a Private Wealth Advisor since 2012. He had previously worked for Penn Square Capital as a director of Institutional Distribution from 2008 to 2010 based in Radnor, PA.

About his academic qualifications, Jeff graduated from West Morris Mendham High School in Mendham, New Jersey. He attended and graduated with BA in International Relations from the University of Pennsylvania. While in college, Jeff played shooting guard for Penn basketball from 2004 to 2004 for coach Dunphy. As a junior he led the nation in three-point shooting, converting 49.3 percent of his attempts from beyond the arc.

Immediately after graduating, Jeff played professional basketball in Europe for three years. He made stops with teams in Portugal, Denmark, and Germany. After three years, he returned to pursue a career in the business world.

During an interview, Jeff shared his experience of playing abroad. He shared, “It was different. It was three years that I’ll look back on with fondness. It was a terrific experience. To be able to see parts of the world that I never thought I’d be able to see and play a sport that I love to play.”

At the University of Pennsylvania, Jeff played 21 games as a freshman, 32 games as a sophomore, 28 games as a junior, and 27 games as a senior. He started 106 games out of 108 games that he played.

New Jersey native was on the NBA draft of 2004 but got undrafted and became an unrestricted free agent. He secured second place in the Top 10 boys basketball scorers in Morris County with 2112 points in 2011.

Also, Jeff earned his MBA from The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania.

Who Are Jeff Schiffner’s Parents And Siblings?

Jeff Schiffner is one of the two children born to his parents: Robert ‘Bob’ Schiffner and Marjorie (nee Wolosin).

His father Bob is a graduate of Morris Hills High School. For college education, he attended Princeton University where he studied statistics. In college, he was a star baseball player for the Tigers. After high school, he played in the New York Yankees organization before pursuing a business career. Bob worked at Nabisco and then with Campbell Soup Company in Camden, N.J., where he was the CFO, retiring in 2008. He lived in Chester for 25 years, where he coached many youth baseball and basketball teams.

Bob and his wife, Marjorie stayed married for 40 years till his death in 2015 at the age of 66.

Jeff has one sister Amy Schiffner. She graduated from Syracuse University in 2001.
