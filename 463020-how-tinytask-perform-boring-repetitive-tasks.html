What is TinyTask?

TinyTaks is a utility software created by Vista software. It is a light application that automates repetitive tasks without requiring macros or learning to write computer codes or scripts. This gives you more time to focus on essential tasks, making you more productive.

The utility software records actions on your computer (mouse and keyboard clicks) and then plays the recording. However, instead of video recording, your computer performs these tasks for you this time. And the good thing is that you can play the recording once, indefinitely, or even a limited number of times.

Recording with TinyTask

You can record tasks with the software in two ways. First, you can press the “Rec” button in the TinyTask window. You can also use the keyboard shortcut “Ctrl+Shift+Alt+R to record.

Once you initiate the recording process, the utility software will record your computer's mouse movements and keyboard presses. This will include all your navigations to a folder, a website, or anywhere else. Next, the software creates a macro that it will use later to automate your tasks.

How to stop TinyTask from recording

Stopping the software from recording is as straightforward as the recording action. All you need to do is to press the same button you did while recording on the multi-user toolbar. Alternatively, you can use the keyboard shortcut “Ctrl+Shift+Alt+R”.

If you want, you can alter the shortcut in the options to suit your preferences. The keyboard shortcut is eliminated from the recording by default.

How to play

Once you have successfully recorded your action, it is time to play and let the software automate your tasks. So, how does it happen?

All you need to do is press the "Play" button once. After you have clicked the button, the software will redo the actions exactly how you recorded them. You can program any repetitive task you desire whenever you want.

Another remarkable feature of the software is that a user can increase the speed of play to complete tasks faster.

How to stop TinyTask from playing

The software is programmed to watch for key presses “Break” or “ScrollLock” or” “Pause” to stop playback. Unfortunately, escape is not a good choice for emergency stops as it is commonly used for other regular interactions during recordings.
