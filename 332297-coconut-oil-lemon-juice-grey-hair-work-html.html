Nobody wants to have grey hair in their early 20s or 30s. That is why many people try as much as possible to take care of their hair to prevent greying. One way they do it is by using coconut oil and lemon juice for grey hair. Does this method work?

Melanin is the hormone that determines the pigmentation of hair. It is responsible for giving hair its colour. But when the melanocyte activity slows down, it can cause greying. Melanocyte activity usually slows down because of things like ageing, hormonal fluctuations, genetic predisposition, poor nutrition, thyroid disorders, harmful hair care products, exposure to the sun’s UV rays, and stress.

If you have black hair, managing it is even more challenging, whether it is in its chemically relaxed or natural state. That is because it has its specific needs, especially when it comes to ensuring enough moisture content. Moisture is crucial to black hair because of its distinctive texture. It nourishes it to help it grow and stay healthy. One natural way of nourishing the hair is to use a mixture of lemon juice and coconut oil.

Can I mix coconut oil with lemon juice?

You can mix coconut oil and lemon juice to get a solution packed with citric acid, lauric acid, vitamin B complex, and flavonoids. All these elements are crucial to the growth and health of black hair. They can also stop premature greying and prevent an oily scalp.

Is lemon good for grey hair?

People have used lemon for a long time as a homemade and natural hair care remedy. Some hair masks and oils also contain lemon. It helps to fight dandruff and promotes hair growth. Lemon is also useful for repressing premature greying of black hair. Furthermore, it has anti-fungal properties that prevent the scalp from getting infections.

Can grey hair be reversed naturally?

Many people know that grey hair is a natural sign of ageing or genetics. While both factors are correct, it is possible to slow down and even reverse the greying process using natural remedies. But to do that, you first need to understand how greying occurs.

As noted above, melanocytes are the pigment cells in the hair follicles that give it its colour. When the body stops producing melanin, the hair starts turning grey. But researchers have also discovered that greying is a build-up of hydrogen peroxide in hair particles. The chemical bleaches the hair from the inside, hence discolouring it.

Usually, it is the catalase enzyme that breaks down the hydrogen peroxide produced in the body into oxygen and water. But as a person ages, the production of the enzyme slows down. As a result, hydrogen peroxide accumulates in the body. It will bleach out the pigment centres of the hair and discolour it. In simple terms, the low production of the catalase enzyme will cause greying.

When applied on the scalp, the vitamins and minerals found in the solution will cleanse the scalp of sebum build-up. They will also stimulate proper blood flow, restore hair shafts, and regenerate new follicles.

Your hair is likely to go grey if it lacks moisture. When combined, lemon juice and coconut oil for grey hair provide moisture to the hair. Black hair is particularly at risk because its natural texture makes it weak in holding onto enough moisture to keep it supple, healthy, and even grow.

How do I use coconut oil and lemon juice on my hair?

A mixture of coconut oil and lemon is essential in stimulating hair growth, fighting dandruff, and reducing itchiness in the scalp. It also makes the hair soft, protects it from sun damage, and prevents it from thinning. But of greater importance is in preventing greying. Here is how to use the mixture to prevent grey hair.

You will need:

  • Three tablespoons of coconut oil
  • One tablespoon of lemon juice

Follow these directions:

  • Mix the coconut oil and lemon juice in a bowl
  • Massage the solution onto your scalp and the hair altogether
  • Leave the treatment for between 30 and 60 minutes
  • After the time elapses, wash off the treatment
  • Use a conditioner made up of coconut oil or shampoo with lemon extract to wash the hair
  • Follow the same routine twice a week for better results
