Monica McNutt has undoubtedly become one of the most renowned sports analysts in the basketball world today, earning recognition for her insightful commentary. She has found a home on prominent networks such as the ACC Network and ESPN, where she serves as a game analyst for ACCN’s basketball coverage.

In addition to her on-court analysis, Monica also shares her expertise as an analyst on information shows like ‘All ACC’ and ‘Nothing But Net’. Monica’s basketball prowess extends back to her college days when she stood out as a remarkable guard for the Georgetown women’s basketball team. Her experience as a player adds depth and firsthand knowledge to her analysis, allowing her to provide unique insights into the game.

Monica McNutt Bio, Age, Height, Parents, Husband, Salary

Monica McNutt was born on 24th October 1989 in Suitland, Maryland, to her lovely parents Kevin McNutt and Desiree McNutt. She grew up with her sister Melanie in Suitland. In the early days of Monica’s life, her father was her coach, both in life as well as when it comes to basketball. Monica played basketball for the majority of her student’s life and she was coached by her father.

During high school, she nurtured her game on the basketball court and gained experience a bit. When it comes to academics, she attended the Academy of the Holy Cross and got her graduation degree from Georgetown University and post-graduation degree from the University of Maryland.

Monica graduated with a BA in the English language from Georgetown University in the year 2011. She got her master’s degree in broadcast journalism from the University of Maryland, College Park in the year 2013.

Monica McNutt Career

Talking about her career she started her professional journey in sports. She played as a standout guard on the Georgetown women’s basketball team from 2007 to 2011. She is a whopping 6 feet, 182cm tall, making her a really hard opponent to play against. Monica helped lead the basketball team to the NCAA Sweet 16 tournament.

A successful and promising basketball career lay in front of Monica but a media and techniques class made her passionate and fall in love with journalism. She got her first internship with NBC4’s sports department. From the year 2011 to 2013, she worked for The Washington Post, head-starting her career.

She was also working for Verizon Fios 1 News during the same time, from 2011 to 2015. She was a member of the NBC Sports Washington and Washington DC area television station (WJLA) in 2015 and the American Sports Network in the year 2017.

Monica was appointed as a lead game analyst by ESPN for their famous ‘Around the Horn’ roundtable discussion show in February 2021. Ever since then, she has been rapidly rising in fame and popularity among all sports enthusiasts and basketball fans around the nation. In an interview, Monica said-

“I think as women, you’re a mom, you’re a sister, you’re a wife and it’s different from being a dad or a husband. As women, we take on so much on top of sports. I think there are some serious stories that need to be told.”
