Early life

Ari was born on March 26, 1991, in Washington, D.C. and spent her early years in the D.C. Metropolitan area. She comes from a large family with six siblings, whom she shares a close bond with. Who is Ari Lennox’s twin sister? There is no record of her having a twin sister or even a twin brother.

Ari Lennox attended Wilson High School. However, before that, she had moved around between several different schools. She ultimately completed her high school education at Duke Ellington School of Arts, focusing on arts and music.

What is Ari Lennox’s nationality? The singer is an American national; she was born in the U.S. to American parents. She is of African-American ethnicity. However, she has revealed that she shares Nigerian ancestry, which she discovered after getting a DNA test.

Another interesting fact about the R&B singer is that she changed her name. Her birth name is Courtney Shanade Salter. So, how did Ari Lennox get her name? After separating from her first professional label contract, she adopted it as her stage name. The name was inspired by The Secret Garden movie character Mary Lennox.

Ari developed a passion for music at a young age. She began writing music at the age of three and started recording in middle school. Her music style and voice developed over the years, thanks to the influence of several artists. Growing up, she listened to artists such as Mariah Carey, Ciara, Minnie Ripperton, Juvenile, Red Hot Chilli Peppers, and Omarion.

Career before and after fame

Ari Lennox had decided she wanted to become a professional singer by the time she was in high school. However, her dream did not come true overnight, and she faced several struggles and rejections. One of them was a failed American Idol audition, which led to her freaking out, smoking weed, and ending up in the hospital.

However, Ari did not give and started recording and uploading her music on the internet and using social media to advertise her music. She also performed at open mics and tried her luck at other singing competitions, though she failed there.

To pay the bills, Ari was forced to work several odd jobs. Some of the places she has worked include Pizza Hut, Wendy’s, Commander Salamander, Whole Foods, and Papa John’s. She also worked as an Uber driver for two years and had a job at public storage.

So, how did Ari Lennox become famous? The key to the R&B singer's success was never giving up. She also pursued opportunities on her own instead of waiting for handouts. In 2013, Ari released her debut mixtape album (Five Finger Discount) and her debut extended play, Ariography.

The following year, she released a single track, Bound, independently. After releasing her debut albums and single, Ari Lennox gained some traction in the music industry. In 2015, she appeared on Elephant Eyes' track, Sweat It Out. Soon, her music started generating waves online, which attracted the attention of American rapper and record producer J. Cole.

On December 8, 2015, Ari was signed by Dreamville Records, becoming the first female artist to be signed by the label. Soon after, she was featured in the track Backseat with American rapper Cozz. This was followed by the release of her new E.P. Pho in 2016. She would also appear on J. Cole’s song, Change, before earning a chance to open for his tour in 2017.

After her career rise, Ari featured on two Dreamville Record songs, Icarus and Nothing but the Best. She then released her first single with the label company, Whipped Cream in 2018. She followed the track with four more singles later in the year, Grampa, 40 Shades of Choke, No One, and Pedigree. These and other tracks were part of her debut album Shea Butter Baby, which she released in 2019.
