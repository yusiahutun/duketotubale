M&amp;S has launched a salted caramel chocolate spread and it sounds amazing

 Posted on April 6, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  444 words  |  Larita Shotwell

If you love salted caramel and chocolate, then you're going to want to head down to your nearest Marks and Spencer as they have launched a new salted caramel product.

The salted caramel chocolate spread is priced at £2.50 and is flavoured with bourbon vanilla, sea salt and caramel.

The sweet condiment has already been a hit on social media, with several snack accounts raving about the delicious sounding spread.

Instagram account Sweet Reviews UK wrote: "We ended up just licking it off the spoon.

"The flavour is something else, it is a sweet, rich almost fudgey toffee caramel, we couldn't pick out the sea salt too much but reflecting on it we think that was a good thing.

"The chocolate part comes in the form of cocoa powder which does come through as an aftertaste which is quite pleasant."⠀

Happily to get you started,  M&S's product developer Trudie Van Niekerk has suggested ten different ways to use the spread.

1. On toast or a toasted bagel
2. As a filling for a cake or swiss roll
3. Added to a brownie mix for decadent salted caramel brownies
4. As a cheesecake topping with some caramelised nuts
5. Instead of toffee in a banoffee pie
6. On top of pancakes or waffles with fruit
7. As a decadent doughnut filling
8. Blended into a milkshake with milk and bananas
9. Sandwiched between biscuits with marshmallows and chocolate to make salted caramel SMORES
10. Enjoyed by the spoonful!

Food blogger Emma Kate has also raved about the product - and handily revealed what 15g converts too if you're following WeightWatchers.

She posted: "I’ve just opened it & tried it (obvs) & it is LUSH! It’s the perfect amount of sea saltyness.

"Ofc this will be going straight on the porridge! Oh my lord I can’t wait! ⁣I haven’t tasted another spread like this so I’m so glad I managed to find it.

"86 calories for 15g ~ If you are doing SW that’s 4.5 syns."

The product isn't available on Ocado yet so you'll have to visit your nearest store to pick up the spread.

The chain is already getting us in the mood for Christmas after launching some festive products.

Customers are going mad for Marks and Spencer's sparkly gin which comes in two flavours; clementine and brand-new rhubarb.

There is also a 23-carat edible gold leaf for the "show-stopping sparkly effect" which is comes in a light-up bottle.
