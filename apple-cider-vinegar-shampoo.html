Is apple cider vinegar good for hair?

The short answer: Yes. While research is scarce on apple cider vinegar’s impact on hair, experts in the field often find it useful to add to their clients’ routines. Lauren Paglionico, hair color specialist and founder of LRN BEAUTY salon in New York City, calls it “one of my favorite all-natural hair tricks. ACV helps balance your scalp pH and remove both dandruff and product buildup. It’s also the secret ingredient to shiny hair.” 

And Justin Anderson, celebrity colorist and co-founder of dpHUE, promises that when using it regularly, “Your hair will be shiny, have less breakage and color fading and your hair will be healthier overall.”

What are the hair benefits of apple cider vinegar?

It removes buildup and increases strand shine

Many of the benefits of apple cider vinegar for hair start with one simple fact: It’s a clarifier. An astringent ingredient, “ACV helps remove product buildup,” says Paglionico. “This is because it’s acidic, and the acid in the ACV breaks down the gunk on your hair.” That means it can blast away dulling leftover particles from hair products and help keep your hair color looking vibrant. As a bonus, Paglionico also says that it “closes the hair cuticle.” Why that matters? “When the cuticle is smooth, it makes your locks super shiny!”

It cleanses hair with anti-bacterial and anti-fungal effects

Apple cider vinegar shampoos are also often touted for helping bust dandruff with its super-cleaning powers. “ACV has both anti-fungal and anti-bacterial benefits,” says Kerry E Yates, a trichologist and founder of beauty brand developing company Colour Collective. This is helpful as she says, “it can help control dandruff as a result of yeast growth.” But even better: “Added to a haircare regimen, it helps support optimal scalp health.”

It encourages healthy hair growth

Read More

Many apple cider vinegar shampoos are suggested for people experiencing hair loss or hair thinning, including the kind that comes naturally with aging. Here’s the thinking behind that: ”By keeping the [hair] follicles clean, and free from product build-up and excess sebum, follicle fitness is improved, thus leading to the optimal environment for new hair growth,” explains Yates. Plus, according to a 2014 study that looked into the pH of shampoos, high acidity formulas like those containing apple cider vinegar may help maintain your scalp’s pH balance, leading to stronger hair that’s less prone to breakage. 

It also has yet another way of making hair look fuller. “The natural chemistry of ACV helps to alleviate excess sebum and eliminates product build-up from both the scalp and hair shaft, says Yates. “The lack of product on the hair shaft can lift up the cuticle making the hair appear fuller.”

How to use apple cider vinegar shampoo

The one thing to note is that since apple cider vinegar is astringent, it can make drier hair types feel parched with overuse. So start by swapping it once a week and then upping it from there if your hair can take it. Yates also suggests, “depending on one’s hair texture, you may need to add a bit of extra conditioner to mid-lengths and ends to enhance slip and shine.”
