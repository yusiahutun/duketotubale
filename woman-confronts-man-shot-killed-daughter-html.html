Woman confronts man who shot her and killed her daughter

 Posted on August 20, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  596 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A woman who was shot and watched her 15-year-old daughter die during a triple murder more than seven years ago finally got the chance to confront her attacker in court on Tuesday.

Camille Hamilton was given the opportunity to address Kevin Pratt in a Miramar, Florida court earlier this week, as he plead guilty to second-degree murder and attempted murder charges for the deadly robbery.

Hamilton and her daughter Nekitta were at Faith Bisa-sor's home in August 2009, when Pratt broke in and shot them all, along with Bisa-sor's 16-year-old son Davion Bishop. Hamilton was the only survivor.

'You leave me to die, but I’m not dead, Kevin Pratt. I am alive. I’m here to tell them what you did to me,' Hamilton said, through tears, according to CBS Miami. 

'I can’t sleep at night without looking in your face. It’s like you’re standing over me, you wish I was dead, but Kevin Pratt, I’m alive and I’m here.'

'This is my child. This was my only child. You took Nekitta. She was only 15,' Hamilton said on Tuesday, while holding a picture of her daughter up to Pratt.

Pratt took a plea deal from prosecutors on Tuesday, agreeing to plead guilty to three counts of second-degree murder and and one count of second-degree attempted murder instead of the first-degree charges he was initially hit with.

Now, instead of the possibility of life in prison, he will get 10 and could be out in just half that time for time already served. 

Prosecutors offered the deal because there were several problems with the case, namely that the strongest piece of evidence was Pratt's DNA on a piece of duct tape used to restrain the victims. 

Hamilton also failed to identify Pratt in a line-up on one occasion.

Just minutes after entering his guilty plea on Tuesday, Pratt made a strange comment while Hamilton was addressing him in court. 

'Tell me you’re sorry, you can’t. You take away everything. Nekitta was only 15! She was a little girl,' Hamilton said. 

'I didn’t do it M’am,' Pratt said

'I don’t want to hear it' Hamilton responded.  

A woman walking out of court on Tuesday with Pratt mother remarked that God 'is a miracle-working God', but Hamilton had a different opinion. 

'You never can look at me and say you’re sorry, you never but God, God is alive and he’s going to punish you,' Hamilton said as she left the stand.
