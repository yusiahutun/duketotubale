THE POPULAR doughnut chain has been serving tasty treats for over 85 years.

Now, customers want to know more about Krispy Kreme, its ownership, and its history.

Who owns Krispy Kreme?

Krispy Kreme is owned by its parent company, JAB Holding Company.

According to its website, JAB is "a unique partnership of long-term investors in consumer goods & services."

It owns multiple other high-profile businesses, such as Panera Bread, Pret A Manger, Espresso House, and Coty.

Michael J. Tattersfield has served as the CEO of Krispy Kreme since January 1, 2017.

When was Krispy Kreme founded?

Krispy Kreme was founded by Vernon Rudolph on July 13, 1937, in Winston-Salem, North Carolina, and is now headquartered in Charlotte.

According to the company's website, Rudolph purchased a "secret yeast raised doughnut recipe" from a New Orleans French chef and rented a building in what is now historic Old Salem in Winston-Salem.

He started by selling doughnuts to local grocery stores before cutting a hole in an outside wall and selling Original Glazed doughnuts "directly to customers on the sidewalk."

In 1989, Krispy Kreme's first retail-only store was born in Greensboro, North Carolina, per its website.

In 2015, the doughnut chain opened its 1,000th store, which is located in Kansas City, Kansas.

How many Krispy Kreme locations are there?

There are about 1,400 Krispy Kreme locations around the world in over 30 countries.
