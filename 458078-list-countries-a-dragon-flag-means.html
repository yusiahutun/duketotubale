List of countries with a dragon on their flag and what it means

A dragon is a legendary creature with serpentine characteristics. It is regarded as a ferocious creature that blows fire from its mouth. This mythical creature has been featured in the legends of several cultures across the globe. There are several countries and cities whose flags feature dragons. So, can you name the countries with a dragon on their flag?

Flags are symbols of states or nations. They represent the traditions and cultures of a nation. You cannot find even a single meaningless part of a flag. One of the most well-known symbols inscribed on flags is the dragon. There has been so much debate about the meaning of the dragon. So, what country is represented by a dragon?

List of countries with a dragon on their flag

How many countries have a dragon on their flag? According to World Atlas, Wales, Bhutan, and Malta are the only countries that have dragons on their flags. Here are all the interesting details you need to know about the meaning of the dragon on these flags.

1. Bhutan

Bhutan is famously known as the Land of the Thunder Dragon. This is because its national flag features a large white dragon in its centre that spreads equally over the dividing line between the two background colours. This Thunder Dragon is the country's emblem.

The history of Bhutan's national flag can be traced back to 1965 when the government officially adopted it. A closer look at the dragon, and you will notice that it grasps jewels. The snarling mouth of the dragon is a symbol of the commitment of Bhutan's deities to defend the country. These jewels are a symbol of national wealth and perfection.

The white colour of the dragon symbolises purity and loyalty. The yellow-orange colour in the background is symbolic of the power of the king, who is the head of government. On the other hand, the orange-red colour is symbolic of the religious commitment of the country.

2. Wales

Wales is a country with a red dragon on its flag. The Welsh people call their flag Y Draig Goch, which translates to a red dragon. This red dragon stands with one foot raised on a green and white background.

It has been associated with Wales for centuries, and the Welsh flag is regarded as one of the oldest flags in human history. Even though the red dragon has been used since around 655AD, it was officially adopted in 1959. The Welsh dragon is a symbol of national pride.

This explains why the images of the Welsh dragon are paraded around during the celebrations to mark St. David's Day. This dragon is symbolic as it signifies valour and bravery. The green and white colours are also symbolic. The white half stands for peace and honesty, while the green colour stands for joy and hope.

3. Malta

The Maltese flag is made up of red and white colours. The origin of these colours can be traced back to the flag of the Knights of Malta which bore a white cross on a red background. If you take a closer look at the Maltese flag, you will notice an image of the George Cross located at the top corner.

In the middle of the George Cross, there is an image of Saint George fighting the dragon. It is believed that Saint George killed the dragon. The George Cross is an award given to Malta by the British King George VI for its bravery in helping the allies in World War II.

There you have it. A comprehensive list of countries with a dragon on their flags. Only three countries in the world have dragons on their flags. The dragon depicted on these flags symbolises bravery, strength, power, and valour.
