The best clogs to buy now and wear for a lifetime

 Posted on June 15, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  645 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Khaite

Khaite

Credit: Net-a-Porter

New York label Khaite’s take on the classic clog offers a seriously versatile style for your wardrobe. A polished staple, the slip-on black design is made from calf leather complete with studs and a multi-toned wooden heel. 

Low in height, it's an easy shoe to run errands in paired with jeans or can be worn in the evening with black trousers, a top and jacket.

Isabel Marant

Isabel Marant

Credit: Matches

Isabel Marant’s take on the clog stays true to the brand’s aesthetic – stylish but never over-thought. 

A simple silhouette, the low-heeled clog features a traditional wooden heel with a creamy leather body held down with flat head pins and simple strap detailing to the front. 

The design comes in jet black as well as chocolate brown.

Givenchy

Givenchy

Credit: Net-a-Porter

Givenchy’s clogs are both slick and elegant with their minimal design. Made from buttery leather, they feature a thick sole, round toe and a logo embellishment on the back heel. 

A style that’s a little smarter than the typical everyday clog, they can be worn to the office as easily as they can a restaurant or cocktail bar. Style with a floaty dress and structured coat or with tailored tailors and a blazer.

Ancient Greek Sandals

Ancient Greek Sandals

Credit: Farfetch

Ancient Greek Sandals’ clogs are handmade in Greece from soft leather and punctuated with gold studs. Complete with a black wooden sole, these have a classic slingback strap fastening.

The studded clogs look excellent styled with opaque tights, a mini skirt, shirt and knitted tank for winter. Come summer, you can wear them with bare legs and a tank top.

Chloé

Chloé

Credit: Net-a-Porter

You can always rely on Chloé to take a style of the 1970s and pay tribute to it while simultaneously giving it a modern feel – and the Joy clogs are proof of that. 

Made with soft suede, they’re lined with luxurious shearling and feature scallop detailing around the toe. The style is also available in black as well as a higher heeled version. Wear with jeans or a floral dress.

Arket

Arket

Credit: Arket

One for the minimalists, Arket’s take on the style is clean and utilitarian. Made in Italy using 100 per cent chrome-free Nappa leather, they’re great everyday clogs with a super comfortable footbed. 

Play to the style and team with a streamlined, pared-back jumpsuit or pair with feminine, romantic garments like a floaty maxi skirt or dress to soften them up.

Gucci

Gucci

Credit: Gucci

Instantly recognisable with the iconic horsebit embellishment, Gucci’s Bamboo-buckle clogs are a classic in the making. Crafted in Italy from calfskin leather, the brand has reimagined old-school clog staples with punchy, eye-catching gold studs that run above the wooden wedge.  

Wear these with anything, anywhere - you’ll certainly get compliments.

Russell and Bromley

Russell and Bromley

Credit: Russell & Bromley

The Russell and Bromley Go Dutch clogs have a modern feel to them with the chunky platform sole and cork footbed. Made from soft black grained nappa leather, they have a classic round toe and gold buckle detailing. 

Also available in bright white, these will add a contemporary edge to any outfit whether you’re out for brunch, running errands or off to the pub.

See By Chloé

See By Chloé

Credit: Net-a-Porter

Made from biscuit suede, See by Chloé technically refer to this design as a slipper but we feel confident categorising it as a clog. A rounded toe, a slip-on style, a sole made for outdoors – it has all the hallmarks of the Dutch shoe, after all. 

Luxurious, soft and featuring a statement logo buckle on the front, these will make you look instantly put together.
