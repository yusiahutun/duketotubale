Upon leaving office, the 43rd president of the United States returned to Midland, Texas, per Miller Center. But what has his daughter been up to since?

What does Jenna Bush Hager do for a living?

Jenna Bush Hager now works as a co-host of NBC’s Today with Hoda & Jenna and an editor-at-large for Southern Living magazine. She wrote The New York Times bestselling book Ana’s Story: A Journey of Hope and is involved in many Unicef projects, states her bio on the Bush Center website.

Who is Jenna Bush Hager’s husband?

Bush Hager married her husband Henry Chase Hager in 2008. Per his bio on the Bush website, Henry is the managing director at Waterous Energy Fund. He previously worked as a staff assistant to the senior adviser at the White House to George while he was president, from 2001-2003.

Bush Hager was the one to propose to her then future hubby, she revealed on Today. “I proposed to Henry after several cocktails,” she said in August. “I was 22 years old, my sister was standing with us, we were dancing and Barbara just started shuffling backwards. She was like, ‘What is happening here?’ We’d only been dating three months. He told me he loved me and I’m like, ‘Me too! Let’s get married!’”

What does Jenna Bush Hager’s twin sister do for a living?

Jenna’s fraternal twin sister Barbara, who also spent time in the White House, has remained much more low-key than some of her other family members. She is known for being an activist, author and doting mother.

How many children does Jenna Bush Hager have?

The Hagers have three children together: Mila, 10, Poppy, seven, and Hal, four. It sounds like she’s already got her hands full with the trio. “I’d like to have another … but no,” she said on Today.
