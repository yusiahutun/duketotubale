Nick Vujicic biography

On December 4, 1982, Nick was born into Dušanka and Borislav Vujičić's family that migrated to Australia from Serbia. His father was shocked by Nick's condition at birth and rushed to the doctor to inquire about his state. The doctor clarified to the father and informed him about the rare condition.

Although Nick’s parents faced a great challenge raising him (the mother did not breastfeed him for the first four months), they gradually got accustomed to his special circumstance. Nick even learnt to skateboard by pushing his body.

The preacher with no arms or legs attended Runcorn State High School in Australia and obtained his high school certificate. He faced a lot of scrutiny at school because of his rare condition and even contemplated suicide at 8 years old.

His parents helped him get through this phase, and Nick was even elected the Captain of MacGregor State in Queensland. He was on the student council that carried out humanitarian work.

How did Nick Vujicic lose his limbs?

Although Nick was healthy in all other aspects during birth, he was born with autosomal recessive tetra-amelia. This is a rare condition that a child lacks limbs except for just two toes. Therefore, he hasn't lost his limbs but was born with a rare condition.

So, is Nick Vujicic a thalidomide baby? It is clear that he was born with a rare condition and not because his mother was taking the drug thalidomide. The drug was heavily used as a sedative and treatment for morning sickness in pregnant women in the late 1950s and early 1960s.
