Castle for Sale in Virginia

 Posted on October 5, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  372 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Have you ever had that sinking feeling that you're meant to do something big with your life, but haven't yet found out what it is?

Well, we think it might be restoring a castle.

Mike Guzman

It's been a Virginia landmark since its construction was completed back in 1860, and now Melrose Castle—a 9,600-square foot, Gothic-Revival style masterpiece framed by 50 acres of (forgive us) enchanted forest—is awaiting its next steward.

Mike Guzman

No, this is not medieval Europe, though it sure seems to be. This is Warrenton, VA, just an hour outside of Washington, D.C. But if the castle seems of another place and time, well, in a way it is. Dr. Murray, the original owner and husband of a shipping heiress, had ancestors in Scotland who lived near Melrose Abbey—the castle's namesake and the setting for many a book by Sir Walter Scott. The couple didn't get to enjoy their little slice of paradise for long, sadly; Upon enlisting in the Confederate Army, Dr. Murray and his wife had left the castle by 1862, after which it became an encampment for Federal troops.

Courtesy of Spring Hill Real Estate, LLC

Little of the once-regal interior has survived the last 156 years, but there's still plenty to work with inside, including crown molding and original mantels. But who cares? The outside, with its three-story octagonal tower and battlemented pediments, is nothing short of spectacular.

Mike Guzman

Mike Guzman

Mike Guzman

Numerous attempts have been made to restore this beloved building to its former grandeur, but Melrose still awaits its knight (lady?) in shining armor.
