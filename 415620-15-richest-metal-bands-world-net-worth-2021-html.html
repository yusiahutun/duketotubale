Heavy metal music is a sub-genre of rock music that was popular in the 1970s and 80s. By the 2000s, the music had lost a great deal of its popularity to rap, hip hop, pop, R&B, and other forms of rock. However, by then, several metal bands had broken into the scene and made a killing. So, is there money in the heavy metal music industry? And if so, which are some of the richest metal bands in the world today?

Some of the music's most essential and popular bands, from Slipknot to Metallica, have made quite the financial name for themselves. They are worth millions of dollars thanks to the many years of hard work, persistence, and unmatched talent in the industry.

1. Metallica – $1 billion

You cannot mention metal music without the name Metallica coming into mind. And there is a good reason for that – it is one of the most successful and richest bands globally.

Metallica is a heavy metal band founded in 1981, around when the genre gained traction. They had a tough start occasioned by rejected recording deals, fights, drugs and alcohol addiction, death, copyright issues, and a critical audience. However, they persevered, becoming one of the highest-selling bands (in all music genres) with over 100 million albums sold. It is thus considered to be one of the best metal bands in the world.

Metallica’s net worth today is estimated to be somewhat around $1 billion. This is the combined net worth of its members: drummer and founding member Ulrich ($350 million), singer and founder Hetfield ($300 - $350 million), Guitarist Hammet ($200 million), and bassist Trujillo ($30 million).

2. Black Sabbath – 140 million

Black Sabbath was a heavy metal band that was founded in the 1960s in the United Kingdom. It was one of the most successful rock groups and is considered among the pioneers of the metal music genre. However, the band went through several tumultuous episodes ranging from addiction issues, change of members, firing, and several reunions.

However, the band was quite successful and has sold over 70 million records over the years. In 2017, founding members Ozzy Osbourne, Geezer Buttler, and Tony Iommi reunited a final time for a farewell tour, after which the band became defunct.

When the Black Sabbath disbanded in 2017, Tommy Iommi was the sole owner of the band. He has a net worth of approximately $140 million.

3. Slayer – $45 million

Slayer is an American metal band that was founded in 1981 in California. It specializes in thrash metal music and a more aggressive sub-genre of metal music. The band has released 12 studio albums and sold over 15 million copies. In 2018, they retired from the scene after a final world tour.

4. Megadeth – $25 million

Megadeth is an American death metal band formed in 1983 by Dave Mustaine, a former Metallica band member. Despite parting on bad terms with Metallica, Mustaine – while at Megadeth – went on to have a successful career. His band sold over 38 million records and produced 15 albums, six of them platinum.

Megadeth is one of the wealthiest death metal bands, with an estimated net worth of around $25 million. This is primarily the combined net worth of David Mustaine ($18 million) and David Ellefson ($5 million) – two of the longest-serving members.

5. Anthrax – $39 million

Anthrax is a heavy metal band that was founded in 1981 by Dan Liker and Scott Ian. It is considered to be among the big five metal bands – the others being Metallica, Slayer, Megadeth, and Exodus.

Anthrax has achieved immense success with over 10 million record sales worldwide. It has also released 11 studio albums (four of them gold), 26 singles, and several other albums and collaborations. The band has an estimated net worth of $39 million.

6. Ozzy Osbourne – $220 million

Ozzy Osbourne is a solo heavy metal band formed by Ozzy Osbourne in 1979 after he was axed from Black Sabbath. Like many other metal bands, it has experienced a revolving door of members, with Osbourne being the only one to feature in all releases.

Ozzy Osbourne is primarily a vehicle for Osbourne’s solo career. It has enjoyed massive success with 12 studio album releases and over 50 million record sales. The band has an estimated net worth of over $220 million (mainly the net worth of the central member, Osbourne).

7. Iron Maiden – $150 million

Iron Maiden is a British metal band formed in 1975 by songwriter and bassist Steven Harris. Despite not in the big 5, it is one of the leading and most iconic metal bands of all time, its success and influence rivalling other top brands such as Metallica, Black Sabbath, and Judas Priest.

Iron Maiden is still active today and enjoys a huge following worldwide. It has released 16 studio albums, 13 live albums, 20 video albums, and several dozen singles. The band has sold over 100 million album copies and over 200 million units for their combined work. It has an estimated combined total net worth of over $150 million, making it one of the richest metal bands in the world.

10. Exodus – $33 million

Exodus is another iconic American thrash metal band that was formed in 1979. It was Kirk Hammet’s former band before he left to join Metallica.

11. Tool – $200 million

The Tool is an American metal band founded in 1990 in Los Angeles. It broke into the scene with heavy metal music but later switched to an alternative metal sub-genre.

Tool has produced five studio albums, one extended play, and one live video album. They have enjoyed massive success with four Grammy Awards win, several world tours, and top charts in several countries. As a result, they have a considerable following, especially in the United States, where they have sold over 13 million album copies.

Tool metal band has an estimated net worth of around $200 million. The figure comes from the combined fortunes of its members: Maynard James Keenan ($60 million), Danny Carey ($50 million), Adam Jones ($50 million), and Justin Chancellor ($45 million).

It is considered to be among the pioneers of nu-metal – a sub-genre of metal music that mixes heavy metal and other forms of music, e.g., hip pop, rap, alternative rock, funk, grunge, etc.
