Rick Fox is a well-known Canadian-Bahamian former basketball player. Rick Fox is famous for playing in the National Basketball Association (NBA) for the Boston Celtics and Los Angeles.

Early Life and Childhood

Rick Fox was born Ulrich Alexander Fox on 24th July 1969. He stepped into this world in Toronto, Ontario, Canada.

With this, he is currently 54 years old and holds Canadian / Bahamian nationality. In addition, he follows the Christian religion and his birth sign is Leo.

Moving on to his family members, he was born to his father Ulrich Fox, and his mother Dianne Gerace. His mother is an Olympic high jumper and also a women’s pentathlete.

Similarly, his father was a Bahamian and his mother was of Italian and Scottish descent.

Rick and his family moved to the Bahamas when he was very young. Likewise, he also has a sister named Jeanene Fox and a brother named Aaron Fox.

Education

In terms of his educational status, he attended Kingsway Academy. Here, he was a member of a high school basketball team named “Saints”.

Career and Professional Life

Rick Fox began his professional basketball career after being picked 24th overall in the first round of the 1991 NBA draft by the Boston Celtics.

He joined the 1992 NBA All-Rookie Second Team as a member of the Celtics.

Likewise, he was released by the Celtics in 1997 and signed with the Los Angeles Lakers. Fox was traded back to the Celtics after the 2003–04 season, but he elected to retire instead.

Fox also represented Canada on the international stage twice; once in 1990 and again in 1994 in the FIBA World Championships.

Moving on, in 1994, he appeared as a member of the Texas Western basketball team in the film “Blue Chips”. Two years later, he played Terry Hastings in the film “Eddie”.

Fox then appeared in the 1998 picture “He Got Game” as the ladies’ guy Chick Deagan.

However, in 1997, he landed the biggest role in his early acting career. On HBO’s prison drama Oz, he portrayed Jackson Vahue, a jail prisoner.
