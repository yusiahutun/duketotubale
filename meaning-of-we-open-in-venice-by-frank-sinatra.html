Meaning of We Open in Venice by Frank Sinatra

 Posted on March 15, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  763 words  |  Aldo Pusey

In the vast world of music, certain songs possess the power to transport us to new realms and immerse us in their unique narratives. One such masterpiece is Frank Sinatra's "We Open in Venice." This iconic tune, composed by Cole Porter for the Broadway musical "Kiss Me, Kate," paints a vivid picture of a traveling troupe of players and their unwavering commitment to the craft of entertainment. While the song may seem lighthearted and whimsical on the surface, it delves into deeper themes of perseverance, determination, and the pursuit of artistic fulfillment.The first verse of the song introduces us to the vibrant band of strolling players, who are not acclaimed stars like those under the wing of the legendary L. B. Mayer. Instead, they are a "simple band who roams about the land, dispensing further of frivolity." This lyric indicates that the troupe seeks not fame or fortune, but rather the simple pleasure of providing a respite from reality through their performances. Here, they embody the theme of dedication to their art, showcasing how their passion for entertainment drives them to continue moving forward despite the challenges they may face.As the song progresses, another standout lyric emerges - "Mere folks who give distraction are we." This line emphasizes the troupe's commitment to offering an escape from the mundanity of everyday life. It suggests that their performances serve as a distraction from the troubles that plague the audience, ranging from personal issues to societal woes. This theme of diversion is crucial, as it underscores the role of art in providing temporary relief from the burdens of the world.Next, the lyrics takes us on a journey through various Italian cities, mirroring the troupe's transient existence. Each city mentioned encompasses distinct cultural or historical significance, which adds a layer of depth to the song. For instance, "We open in Venice" captures the grandeur and allure of this iconic city built on a lagoon. It symbolizes the troupe's aspirations, their desire to commence their artistic endeavors in a place renowned for its rich history and artistic heritage. This theme of ambition can be extrapolated to our own lives, reminding us to pursue our dreams in environments that elevate our passions.Moving on, "Lots of laughs in Cremona" and "Lots of money in Cremona" bring forth the themes of joy and prosperity. Cremona is renowned for being the birthplace of some of the finest stringed instruments, particularly violins. By associating laughter and wealth with Cremona, the lyrics suggest that these intangible and material rewards can be harnessed through the troupe's artistic endeavors. It implies that the pursuit of their craft can bring both financial stability and emotional fulfillment, culminating in a life full of laughter and prosperity.In contrast, the mention of Parma conjures up an unexpected and imaginative theme. "That dopy mopie menace" hints at the challenges and hurdles that the troupe encounters in this city. Interestingly, the use of the word "menace" suggests that these obstacles, despite their troublesome nature, are an integral part of the troupe's journey. They force the players to adapt, to think on their feet, and ultimately grow as performers. In a broader sense, this theme encourages us to embrace and conquer the difficulties we encounter, recognizing them as catalysts for personal and professional development.The lyrics continue to navigate through Mantua and Padua, cities steeped in history and culture. Mantua, known for its architectural marvels and Renaissance art, symbolizes the inspiration that the troupe draws from its surroundings. This theme underscores the transformative power of artistic environments, serving as sources of motivation and propelling the troupe forward. Padua, on the other hand, represents the culmination of their journey, highlighting their unwavering commitment to their craft. It signifies their ability to overcome the challenges faced in previous cities and the anticipation of yet another triumphant opening night.Throughout "We Open in Venice," the underlying theme of perseverance stands tall amidst the joy, ambition, and challenges encountered by the troupe. They navigate the winding path of their artistic journey, propelled by an unyielding dedication to providing respite, distraction, and entertainment to those they encounter. Their steadfast commitment to their craft serves as an inspiration to us all, encouraging the pursuit of our own passions, no matter the obstacles that lie in our way.As the final notes of the song ring out, the troupe bids adieu to their current location, venturing onward to their next destination. This, in essence, encapsulates the timeless theme of the never-ending artistic journey, an odyssey constantly beckoning us to open doors, explore uncharted territories, and discover the boundless possibilities that lie beyond.
