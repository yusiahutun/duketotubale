Karanja Kibicho Hands Over State Department of Interior: &quot;Don't Call Me PS Moving Forward&quot;

 Posted on April 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  488 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • Karanja Kibicho and Fred Matiang'i were on the spot during campaigns as the Kenya Kwanza Alliance accused them of using government machinery to decampaign them
  • Matiang'i was replaced by Kithure Kindiki while Kibicho position has been taken by Raymond Omollo
  • Omollo expressed gratitude to the outgoing PS and staff for supporting him before vetting and for setting the bar high in service delivery to Kenyans

The Principal Secretary, State Department Of Interior and Citizen Services in the Ministry of Interior Karanja Kibicho has handed over to Raymond Omollo.

In a handover ceremony that was held at the Harambee House on Monday, December 5, Kibicho handed over his office and other instruments to his successor Raymond Omollo.

Kibicho handed over a report on the Inventory Asset Register, files on Semi-Autonomous Government Agencies (SAGAs), Internal Affairs and reports from each region on security and data.

"Don't call me PS moving forward, I have already handed over my roles as PS to Raymond Omollo for the State Department for Migration Citizen Services that deals with the security of the nation," Kibicho said.

The PS disclosed that he was able to stop a bad habit at Nyayo House where Kenyans were asked to pay a certain amount before they received their Identification Cards

"During my tenure at this department, I have weeded out cartels at Nyayo House. If you walk around, you won't find any individuals asking for something before processing your ID or passport," he explained.

Kibicho welcomed Omollo and assured him that he will always be available for consultations even though the later now has full autonomy of the docket.

"I want to commit to you that I will not interfere with your staff. You are now the one in charge but that does not mean we cannot have a cup of tea," he said.

Raymond Omollo appreciates Kibicho, staff

On his side, the newbie thanked the outgoing PS for immense support and promised to maintain above par services to Kenyans. A record he said was set by his predecessor.

"What he is handing over to me is a robust state department that has achieved a lot all over the country and the challenge is to take it to the next level," Omollo explained.

He expressed gratitude to staff for helping him prepare for vetting beforehand and providing everything he required.

"We sat in this building a few weeks ago and you were able to take me through some of the important things I needed to know before appearing to before parliament," Omollo disclosed.

The incoming Omollo requested for cooperation from all the staff as he sets out to do his mandate and push Kenya Kwanza Alliance agenda.
