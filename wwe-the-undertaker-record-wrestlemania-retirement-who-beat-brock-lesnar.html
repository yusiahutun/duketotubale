What was The Undertakers record at WrestleMania and who did he beat? WWE icon's legendary streak

 Posted on February 21, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  532 words  |  Larita Shotwell

WrestleMania takes place over the next two nights and the Undertaker remains the most synonymous name with the event years after his retirement.

In professional wrestling terms, Taker's undefeated WrestleMania record was the most sacred in the business that ran for over two decades.

It became an attraction within itself when fans would clamour to see which wrestler could break the Deadman's legendary run.

At WrestleMania 13, WWE Hall of Famer Jim Ross finally acknowledged the run and the legend continued to grow.

What is The Undertaker’s WrestleMania record?

The Phenom competed in a record 27 WrestleMania matches during his storied career - four more than Triple H in second.

His WrestleMania record is 25-2.

Undertaker's legendary streak at the Show of Shows began at WrestleMania VII in 1991 when he beat Jimmy Snuka.

It ran for over 20 years in which the Deadman only missed two Mania's through injury and defeated 18 men during the Streak.

Undertaker's reign finally came to an end at the hands of Brock Lesnar at WrestleMania XXX in 2014.

He went onto have five more matches at the annual event, losing once again to Roman Reigns at WrestleMania 33.

What has been said?

Taker lifted the lid on how the decision was made during a documentary exploring the relationship between himself and his on-screen brother Kane.

“I got to the arena that day and I was going over,” he said.

“Right. We had gone back and forth and when I showed up I was going over and through the course of the day, Vince came to me and said, he changed his mind. And I was like, okay?

“I said, well, I don’t think Brock needs it, but at the end of the day, it’s your show, it’s your call.

“If you’re sure about it, that’s what we’ll do.

“And that’s part of the business, obviously. That’s what we do. I thought there would be somebody like Roman [Reigns]. I think Roman would have been fine.”
