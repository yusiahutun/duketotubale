Camilla recycles Anna Valentine coat dress she wore to Meghan and Harry's wedding for Royal Ascot

 Posted on July 16, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1553 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Queen Camilla was a vision in pink as she attended the second day of the Royal Ascot this afternoon. 

The royal, 75, looked effortlessly elegant in a V-necked pastel piece of outerwear, which she first wore to Prince Harry and Meghan Markle's wedding in 2018.

The sophisticated garment is by British designer Anna Valentine and features fringing on the front and lapel. 

Recreating the outfit she wore to her stepson's Windsor nuptials, Camilla paired her summery coat dress with a five-stranded pearl necklace with a large pink topaz in the middle.

The antique item was originally an Edwardian-era brooch and is said to have been purchased at auction by the Royals in 2000.

However, the newly-crowned Queen did slightly alter the outfit she first debuted in May 2018 by changing up her hat for today's event.

The royal opted for camel-toned headpiece by her favourite designer Philip Treacy, which designed her wedding headpiece in 2005 and the feathered style she to Prince Harry and Meghan's wedding.

Camila was also pictured wearing a Van Cleef & Arpels Alhambra bracelet in blue.

The stylish royal completed her ensemble with a nude clutch bag and taupe suede heels. 

The King and Queen arrived at Royal Ascot today in a horse-drawn carriage with the Earl and Countess of Halifax.

The Countess of Halifax was formerly married to Andrew Parker Bowles' brother Richard, which makes her Camilla's ex-sister-in-law. 

The two couples were seen happily talking amongst themselves as they arrived at the event and waved to the crowds.

This year, organisers of the race have paid tribute to Royal Ascot's 'biggest fan', Queen Elizabeth II, who passed away last September.

During her 70-year reign, Her late Majesty attended almost every single Royal Ascot festival, with the exception of last year.

This year, organisers have displayed a heartwarming collection of photos of the Queen visiting Ascot over the years. 

They include a black-and-white photo of the royal riding on the tracks in 1960 when she was 34-years-old. 

The mother-of-four was pictured with a headscarf and riding boots on on as hwe horse galloped along the track.

The Queen was taking part in an unofficial race with others members of her royal party and ended up finishing fourth. 

What's more, the organisers also selecting a photograph of the Queen beaming at Ladies Day in 2013. 

The event turned out to be one of the Queen's most successful Ascots as her horse Estimate won the Gold Cup - making her the first monarch in 200 years to scoop the prize.

The late royal was pictured beaming at her trophy, which was presented to her by her son Prince Andrew. 

The Queen was dressed in one of her signature coat dresses in a pretty shade of purple. 

Underneath her jacket, the monarch wore a pretty floral dress - which perfectly complimented the flowers on her hat. 

In the image, the Queen is seen wearing a three-strand pearl necklace - which she also wore to her final ever Ascot in June 2021.
